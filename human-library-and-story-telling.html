Story Telling

The TATA Steel Foundation set up a ‘Human Library’, not a collection of books, but a living archive of extraordinary lives—a collection of people who have and continue to create immense impact in their communities.

In this setup, eight people became open books and shared their fascinating chapters of triumph, adversity, and resilience.

Women leaders from different sectors shared personal stories of resilience and empowerment, showcasing the potential of individuals like Latha, Bobby, and Priyanka to become community leaders despite facing numerous obstacles. The session highlighted the importance of social security, resilience, support and collaboration between government, businesses, civil society, factories and workforce representatives to drive positive change and create equitable workplaces. The next session segues into discourse by representatives of UN Women, ILO, private sector and civil society on solutions at systemic, behavioural and practice levels that needs to be addressed to achieve workforce development and well-being.

Key takeaways focused on the significance of investing in women’s empowerment, infrastructure, and capacity building to foster community development. The need for responsible business leadership and practices, ethical sourcing and inclusive policies to support women’s economic participation and address vulnerabilities like gender-based violence, discrimination, climate change and occupational safety were emphasised.

The discussions underscored the complex interplay of economic, social, and environmental factors influencing the workforce, calling for a more human-centric approach to business and policy-making. The session concluded with a call to action for strengthening labour policies, improving investments, demonstration of leadership, deepening evidence based results promoting gender equality by fostering collaboration between diverse stakeholders to create a more inclusive and sustainable future for all.

Fueling Growth

Dr. R Balasubramaniam took centre stage to shed light on the essential role of the Capacity Building Commission of India (CBCI) and the transformative journey from a Karmachari to a Karmayogi. He emphasised the critical need to understand the state’s background and the evolving landscape post-independence, where provisioning shifted towards a more provisional approach in the 90s.

Dr. Balu highlighted the importance of fostering collaboration, accountability, and citizen-centric services within the civil service framework. The discussions delved into competency frameworks for creating a just and equitable Vikshit Bharat, focusing on self-reflection, collaboration with ministries and NGOs, policy formulation, and effective service delivery.

A standout moment was Dr. Balu’s reflection on equity and equality as the core foundation for education, proposing that the ultimate goal is for the Capacity Building Commission to become redundant in the future. His vision entails setting up a robust Capacity Building unit within the state over the next decade, emphasising the need for active participation, engagement, and a shift towards collaborative problem-solving approaches.

In closing, Dr. Balu’s inspiring quote resonated, ““If we had equity and equality as a foundation for the education we get, Capacity Building Commission would not exist. I hope we become redundant after 10 years we are setting Capacity Building unit with the state” .

This session provided a comprehensive discussion on the critical aspects of effective skill development programs and sustainable impact creation. It underscored the significance of collaboration among corporates, NGOs, social enterprises, and government entities to drive meaningful change.

Understanding and adapting to evolving youth aspirations emerged as a key factor shaping successful skill development initiatives. The integration of AI and digital technologies was highlighted as a powerful tool to enhance skill-building efforts, particularly in rural areas, opening new avenues for growth and innovation.

Emphasising a holistic approach, the session stressed the need to address various facets such as education, infrastructure, and life skills to foster comprehensive development outcomes. Building sustainable market linkages, particularly in agriculture-focused projects, was identified as crucial for long-term success and community resilience. The discussion also noted the importance of focusing on localised solutions tailored to address specific community needs, ensuring interventions are impactful and sustainable in the long run.

Overall, the session illuminated the essential components for effective skill development programs and sustainable impact strategies, advocating for collaboration, innovation, and community-centred approaches in tackling development challenges.

Wicked Solutions

The Wicked Solutions Show’ is a podcast hosted by Shiv Kumar — Co-founder of The Catalyst Group and Chief Integrator of the COVIDActionCollab.

The show brings to light the ‘wicked problems’ faced by the social development sector and the challenges they overcome to solve it! Wicked problems are indeed difficult to solve: they’re complex, incomplete, and contradictory. Times like this call for solutions that are cooperative, creative and adaptable.
